Featured Projects

    Royal Ghost: Clash Royale AI Agent (Spring 2022)

    (Pictured) The gameboard in both visual representations.

  • Goal: Train an Artificial Intelligence agent to play Clash Royale rationally and effectively.
  • Findings: Using reinforcement learning (Q-learning), the agent was able to learn when to use key cards in just over 5,000 games.
  • The gameplay was simplified by making all turns occur within one update pass every second, scaled to be accurate to the real game, and this allowed for training with full games at 100x speed.
  • The Q-Learner agent learned by playing successive games against an equivalent agent with older weights, updating the weight of each Q-value after each turn concluded.
  • The Q-Learner agent was biased toward cheaper cards with more easily verifiable Q-values, and learned how to properly counter different types of cards (air units, tank units) by playing against itself.
  • Next, a neural network based agent will be trained to play against the Q-learner, and the agents will be playable opponents for a human user.
    Sand Simulator Engine (Spring 2022)

    (Pictured) Sand falling over four spheres with realistic physics.

  • Goal: Simulate a large number of sand particles with physically realistic forces, including inter-particle and wind forces, in real time.
  • Findings: This was possible by using GLSL, C++, implementing Verlet integration, cleverly using step size and integration clipping, and the addition of offline rendering as well.
  • Working from the CS184 ClothSim skeleton, we implemented sand particles, particle physics, textures and shading, integration over time, offline rendering, wind forces, dynamic force calculations, and real-time display.
    CS184 Graphics Portfolio (Spring 2022)

    (Pictured) A cornell box + spheres rendering with our ray tracing engine.

  • Overview: A portfolio of graphics projects completed in C++ for CS184.
  • Rasterizer - a rasterizer for simple polygons, rendering SVG images to the screen pixel by pixel.
  • MeshEdit - a triangle mesh editor engine, with the ability to edit CODA files and perform Loop Subdivision to upsample 3d meshes.
  • PathTracer - a ray tracing engine for global illumination, including multi-bounce illumination, accurate tracing of trajectories, and importance sampling for efficiency.
  • ClothSim - a spring-based physical simulator for cloths and 3d objects, with object collision, Blinn-Phong shading, environment map shading, and the ability to use custom textures.

    SAAS Sexual Education Research Paper (Spring 2021)

    (Pictured) The statistical significance of 'Presentation of Sexuality' in State Sex. Ed. Curriculum.

  • Research Question: How have State-wide Sexual Education policies of the last 20 years affected the prominence of LGBTQ+ communities across the United States?
  • Findings: the representation of sexuality in the classroom has a statistically significant relationship with LGBTQ+ representation in that State, and more informative policy generally correlated with higher LGBTQ+ representation.
  • I employed a variety of modeling techniques on US census and sexual education public data, and I worked to assess causes and causality.
  • Using Python (Pandas, Numpy, Sklearn), PCA proved especially helpful, as did trying to create hyperfeatures from the largely binary data (i.e. implemented a safe-sex policy or omitted such a policy).
